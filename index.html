The Lab Chronicles

Explore the details of three key experiments in chemistry!

1. Determination of Hardness of water sample

This experiment uses the EDTA titration method to measure the hardness of water by identifying the concentration of calcium and magnesium ions.

  • Objective: Analyze water quality by measuring hardness.
  • Tools: EDTA solution, Erlenmeyer flask, burette.
  • Outcome: Evaluate the suitability of water for various applications.

2. Determination of alkalinity of Water sample

This practical involves determining the water sample's alkalinity, indicating its buffering capacity.

  • Objective: Analyze water quality by measuring alkalinity.
  • Tools: Water sample, Buffer solution, burette.
  • Outcome: Evaluate the alkalinity of water for various applications.

3. Determination of the Dissociation Constant (Ka) of Acetic Acid

This experiment involves using a pH meter to determine the dissociation constant of acetic acid by analyzing pH changes at various dilutions. It helps understand the strength of weak acids.

  • Objective: Calculate the Ka of acetic acid.
  • Tools: pH meter, acetic acid, standard solutions.
  • Outcome: Develop insights into equilibrium chemistry.

4. Titration of Strong Acid vs. Strong Base

Using a conductivity meter, this experiment monitors the titration of a strong acid (e.g., HCl) with a strong base (e.g., NaOH). The conductivity of the solution is used to determine the equivalence point and the strength of the acid.

  • Objective: Conduct titration using conductivity as an indicator.
  • Tools: Conductivity meter, strong acid, strong base.
  • Outcome: Accurately determine the acid strength.
